20-Week Study Plan

JLPT N4 · Genki II (Lessons 13–23) — Target: Saturday 5 December 2026

on-track

Daily Routine Templates

Weekday (45–60 min)
  • 5–10 min: flashcard / Anki review (vocab + kanji)
  • 25–35 min: Genki II chapter work — grammar + textbook exercises
  • 10–15 min: listening (Genki II audio / podcast)
Weekend (1.5–2 hours)
  • 10–15 min: Anki review
  • 40–50 min: workbook exercises + writing practice
  • 20 min: reading (NHK Easy / graded readers)
  • 20 min: listening practice
  • 30 min: mock-exam sections (from the review phase)
Phase 1 · Genki II — textbook & workbook (Weeks 1–16)

▶ Week 1: Lesson 13 — Potential Verbs (Jul 20 – Jul 26)

Focus: Ease into Genki II. Lesson 13 grammar + textbook exercises.

  • Grammar: potential verbs (食べられる／飲める), 〜し, 〜そうです (looks like), 〜てみる, なら, frequency (週に◯回)
  • Textbook: read the dialogue, do all in-chapter grammar exercises
  • Kanji: start Lesson 13 kanji set (~15 new)
  • Vocab: add Lesson 13 words to your deck — aim 8–10/day
Checkpoint: Can say what you're able/unable to do and give reasons with 〜し. ~55 Genki II vocab.

▶ Week 2: Lesson 13 Workbook + Consolidate (Jul 27 – Aug 02)

Focus: Finish Lesson 13 completely — workbook, listening, kanji.

  • Workbook: all Lesson 13 pages (grammar + listening comprehension)
  • Lock in the Lesson 13 kanji (reading + meaning)
  • Do the Genki 13 listening tracks — pause and shadow
  • Weekend: quick self-test on 〜そう vs 〜みたい and potential forms
Checkpoint: Lesson 13 done end-to-end. Potential verbs automatic. 15 kanji.

▶ Week 3: Lesson 14 — Wants & Giving/Receiving (Aug 03 – Aug 09)

Focus: Lesson 14 textbook + workbook in one week.

  • Grammar: ほしい, 〜かもしれません, あげる／くれる／もらう, 〜たらどうですか
  • Drill the giving/receiving triangle — who gives to whom matters
  • Textbook + workbook exercises; Lesson 14 kanji (~15)
  • Vocab: 8–10/day
Checkpoint: Can express wants and describe gifts/favours in both directions. ~110 vocab, ~30 kanji.

▶ Week 4: Lesson 15 — Volitional & Intentions (Aug 10 – Aug 16)

Focus: Lesson 15 textbook + workbook.

  • Grammar: volitional form (行こう／食べよう), volitional + と思っています, 〜ておく, 〜という
  • Drill volitional conjugation for all three verb groups
  • Textbook + workbook; Lesson 15 kanji (~16)
  • Weekend: write 5 sentences about your plans using 〜と思っています
Checkpoint: Can state intentions and say you'll do something in advance. ~165 vocab, ~46 kanji.

▶ Week 5: Lesson 16 — Favors & Conditionals (part 1) (Aug 17 – Aug 23)

Focus: Lesson 16 textbook — the favor verbs are dense, give them room.

  • Grammar: 〜てあげる／てくれる／てもらう (doing favours), 〜ていただけませんか, 〜と (conditional)
  • This is where giving/receiving + te-form combine — drill slowly
  • Textbook exercises; begin Lesson 16 kanji (~15)
Checkpoint: Can ask for and describe favours politely. ~200 vocab.

▶ Week 6: Lesson 16 Workbook + Lesson 17 start (Aug 24 – Aug 30)

Focus: Close out Lesson 16, open Lesson 17.

  • Lesson 16 workbook + listening; finish Lesson 16 kanji
  • Start Lesson 17 grammar: 〜そうです (hearsay), 〜って, 〜たら
  • Note the difference: 〜そう (looks like, L13) vs 〜そう (I hear, L17)
Checkpoint: Lesson 16 complete. ~230 vocab, ~77 kanji.

▶ Week 7: Lesson 17 — Hearsay & たら (Aug 31 – Sep 06)

Focus: Finish Lesson 17 textbook + workbook.

  • Grammar: 〜なくてもいいです, 〜みたいです, 〜前に／〜てから
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 17 kanji (~16)
  • Compare all your conditionals so far: なら / と / たら
Checkpoint: Comfortable reporting what you heard and sequencing events. ~285 vocab.

▶ Week 8: Catch-up & Review — Lessons 13–17 (Sep 07 – Sep 13)

Focus: Breather week. Consolidate the first five chapters — no new grammar.

  • Re-do any workbook sections that felt shaky
  • Full review of the three conditionals + giving/receiving + potential/volitional
  • Kanji + vocab clean-up: get everything from L13–17 to solid
  • Mini self-test: mixed grammar from L13–17
  • If you're ahead, get a head start on Lesson 18
Checkpoint: First 5 Genki II chapters genuinely solid. ~285 vocab, ~93 kanji. Buffer used or banked.

▶ Week 9: Lesson 18 — Transitivity & てしまう (Sep 14 – Sep 20)

Focus: Lesson 18 textbook + workbook.

  • Grammar: transitivity pairs (自動詞・他動詞), 〜てしまう, 〜と (whenever), 〜ながら, 〜ばよかった
  • Make a transitivity-pair table (あく/あける, つく/つける, きえる/けす…) and drill it
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 18 kanji (~16)
Checkpoint: Transitive/intransitive pairs no longer trip you up. ~340 vocab, ~109 kanji.

▶ Week 10: Lesson 19 — Honorifics (Sep 21 – Sep 27)

Focus: Lesson 19 textbook + workbook — keigo begins.

  • Grammar: honorific (respectful) verbs, giving respectful advice, 〜てくれてありがとう, 〜てよかった, 〜はずです
  • Memorise the irregular honorifics: いらっしゃる, 召し上がる, ご覧になる…
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 19 kanji (~16)
Checkpoint: Can use respectful language and say what's supposed to happen. ~395 vocab, ~125 kanji.

▶ Week 11: Lesson 20 — Humble Speech (Sep 28 – Oct 04)

Focus: Lesson 20 textbook + workbook — the other half of keigo.

  • Grammar: extra-modest & humble expressions, 〜ないで, questions within larger sentences (〜か／かどうか), name という, 〜やすい／〜にくい
  • Pair humble vs honorific side by side (伺う vs いらっしゃる, etc.)
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 20 kanji (~16)
Checkpoint: Keigo (both directions) under control. ~450 vocab, ~141 kanji.

▶ Week 12: Catch-up & Review — Lessons 18–20 (Oct 05 – Oct 11)

Focus: Second buffer week. The keigo chapters are dense — let them settle.

  • Re-drill honorific + humble verb tables until instant
  • Mixed review of L18–20 grammar and kanji
  • Vocab clean-up; clear any red/amber cards
  • Optional first look at Lesson 21 (passive) if you're ahead
Checkpoint: 8 of 11 chapters done and solid. ~450 vocab, ~141 kanji.

▶ Week 13: Lesson 21 — Passive (受身) (Oct 12 – Oct 18)

Focus: Lesson 21 — one of the big three. Textbook + workbook.

  • Grammar: passive sentences (〜られる), 〜てある, 〜間に, adjective + する
  • Drill passive conjugation for all verb groups; watch the に (agent) particle
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 21 kanji (~16)
Checkpoint: Can form and understand passive sentences. ~505 vocab, ~157 kanji.

▶ Week 14: Lesson 22 — Causative (使役) (Oct 19 – Oct 25)

Focus: Lesson 22 — causative. Textbook + workbook.

  • Grammar: causative sentences (〜せる／させる), 〜ていただけませんか, verb stem + なさい, 〜ば (conditional), 〜のに
  • Contrast causative (make/let do) with passive from last week
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 22 kanji (~16)
Checkpoint: Can say you make/let someone do something, and use the ば-conditional. ~560 vocab, ~166 kanji.

▶ Week 15: Lesson 23 — Causative-Passive (使役受身) (Oct 26 – Nov 01)

Focus: Final Genki II chapter — the hardest form. Textbook + workbook.

  • Grammar: causative-passive (〜させられる), 〜ても, 〜ことにする, 〜ことにしている, 〜まで, 〜方 (かた)
  • Build the passive → causative → causative-passive ladder in one table
  • Workbook + listening; Lesson 23 kanji (final ~16)
Checkpoint: 🎉 Genki II complete. All 11 chapters + all N4 kanji done. ~615 vocab, ~173 kanji.

▶ Week 16: Full Genki II Review + Mock #1 (Nov 02 – Nov 08)

Focus: Consolidate the whole book and take a baseline mock.

  • One pass through the full Genki II grammar checklist — flag anything weak
  • Vocab + kanji consolidation: everything to green
  • Weekend: take your FIRST full N4 mock exam (timed). Score it honestly.
  • Record which sections (vocab / grammar / reading / listening) are weakest
Checkpoint: Book finished. Mock #1 done — you now know exactly what to fix.
Phase 2 · Review & mock exams (Weeks 17–20)

▶ Week 17: Weak-Spot Assault + Mock #2 (Nov 09 – Nov 15)

Focus: Attack whatever Mock #1 exposed.

  • Rank your weakest areas from Mock #1 and spend most sessions there
  • Grammar weak? Re-drill those Genki chapters' exercises
  • Deep review of the grammar checklist — every point to a tick
  • Weekend: Mock #2 — compare section scores to Mock #1
Checkpoint: Mock #2 shows clear improvement. Gap areas narrowing.

▶ Week 18: Reading & Listening Focus + Mock #3 (Nov 16 – Nov 22)

Focus: The two sections people underprepare for.

  • Reading: one N4-level passage per day, timed — build reading speed
  • Listening: full N4 listening sections back-to-back, 30 min/day
  • Timed section drills for each JLPT part under pressure
  • Weekend: Mock #3 under strict conditions
Checkpoint: Reading speed and listening comfort up. Scoring 65%+ on mocks.

▶ Week 19: Exam Simulation Mode (Nov 23 – Nov 29)

Focus: Full-length, real-conditions practice. Build stamina and timing.

  • Full timed mock exam(s) — no phone, no pausing
  • Vocab/kanji: maintenance only (reviews, no new cards)
  • One quick pass through all Genki II grammar points
  • Fix the recurring mistakes that keep showing up across mocks
Checkpoint: Scoring 70%+ comfortably. Timing is second nature.

▶ Week 20: Final Week — Stay Sharp, Don't Cram (Nov 30 – Dec 05)

Focus: Light review, confidence, rest before exam day.

  • Mon–Wed: light reviews + one listening session per day. No new material.
  • Thu: quick scan of the grammar checklist — refresh tricky points
  • Fri (Dec 4): one light practice section if you want, then relax. Prep materials (pencils, eraser, watch, photo ID). Early night.
  • Sat (Dec 5): EXAM DAY. Trust your preparation — you've done the work.
Checkpoint: Ready. ~615 vocab, 173 kanji, all Genki II grammar, 4 mocks behind you. Go get N4.
