The three test sections

N4 is sat in three timed parts, in this order (short breaks between them):

OrderSection (Japanese)ContentsTime
1言語知識（文字・語彙）
Language Knowledge		Vocabulary: kanji readings, orthography, word choice, usage25 min
2言語知識（文法）・読解
Language Knowledge & Reading		Grammar + reading passages55 min
3聴解
Listening		Listening comprehension35 min

Roughly 115 minutes of testing in total.

Note: the JLPT occasionally adjusts section timing. Confirm the exact minutes for your test date on the official site before exam day.

How it's scored

Your marks are grouped into two scoring categories, not three:

Scoring categoryScore rangeSectional minimum
Language Knowledge (vocab + grammar) & Reading0 – 12038
Listening0 – 6019
Total0 – 18090

To pass you need total ≥ 90 and each category above its minimum. Ace vocab/grammar but bomb listening (under 19) and it's still a fail — so listening can't be neglected.

Question types to expect

Vocabulary (文字・語彙)

  • Kanji reading — pick the hiragana reading of a kanji word
  • Orthography — pick the kanji for a word written in kana
  • Context — choose the word that fits the blank
  • Paraphrase — pick the sentence closest in meaning
  • Usage — choose the sentence where a word is used correctly

Grammar (文法)

  • Grammar form — choose the correct particle/ending for the blank
  • Sentence composition — reorder ★ pieces into a correct sentence
  • Text grammar — fill blanks so a short passage flows

Reading (読解)

  • Short passages (~150 chars) — a single comprehension question
  • Medium passages (~450 chars) — a few questions
  • Information retrieval — find details in a notice, ad, or schedule

Listening (聴解)

  • Task-based — what should the person do next?
  • Point comprehension — listen for a specific piece of information
  • Verbal expressions — choose the right thing to say in a situation
  • Quick response — pick the natural reply to a short line

What N4 assumes you can do

N4 certifies you can understand basic Japanese used in everyday situations: reading passages on familiar topics written in basic vocabulary and kanji, and following slow, everyday conversation. In numbers, roughly:

  • ~300 kanji and ~1,500 words (Genki I + II gets you there)
  • All the Genki II grammar — potential, conditionals, passive, causative, keigo basics
Strategy: the two buffer weeks and 4-week review phase in your study plan are built around this scoring — mock exams surface which of the two categories is dragging, so you can defend the 38 / 19 minimums well before December.

Format & scoring per the official JLPT guidelines (jlpt.jp) and current N4 test-format guides. Always reconfirm on jlpt.jp for your exam sitting.
